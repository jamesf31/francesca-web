    We were sitting on the stump at the top of the hill -- the stump with enough rings that it's hard to count at a glance. A tree that may have been felled years ago or may been felled yesterday or might as well still be standing. I remember the clouds rolling on top of each other in white and the lighter shades of gray, and gray and the darker shades of white, and the pines whispering with all of the words of the people we love that are dead and the people we love that are alive but far away and the people that we can't understand.
    <br>
    <br>
    But most of all I remember the sun that day and the way that my hands knew that they were in the sun because it had been foggy all morning but now the sun was shining straight through from the heavens and my hands could tell that they were lucky to be feeling the light and the heat that in the scheme of it all darkness and night is the default and we are lucky to be in the sun at all and let alone this immense quality of light and heat that breeches all of the darkness of all of the space in between us and the sun and reaches down and lifts us and lets our hands know that they are now in the sun in the heat in the light.
    <br>
    <br>
    And the tips of the pines, just the tips of them, sticking out above the horizon of the hill and waving in the breeze. Whispering, I guess as I said, but just the tips of them, the last bits of the branches, sticking up over the horizon of the hill and onto the blankest bluest sheet of sky with ripples and gradients and the smallest black flocks of birds soaring in groups and apart and in groups and apart but following one path over ages and ages and ages. And beneath us the town and the fields, the cows breathing and the horses breathing and the cars breathing. And the people in and out of the houses. And the houses breathing. And the cows walking in the fields and the grass swaying in the wind each blade swaying in its own way but each field swaying together and all the fields swaying at once. And across the valley the other hills, the greater hills, the houses fewer and further between, the pines greater and tighter between, not now felled like on this hill.
    <br>
    <br>
    And so yes we were sitting on the great stump on the hill in the light from the heavens that only sometimes decides to reach down and touch our hands.
    <br>
    <br>
    I had been there many times before alone and sometimes even under the great light but this time it was with you and our fingers were intertwining and then I was kissing you and then I was holding you and then I was looking at your eyes in the sun and then all of a sudden there's a great tugging on the crowns of our head and the tops of our chest and it feels strong but not uncomfortable and then I'm holding you and I can feel that I'm no longer making contact with the stump just with you and with the air and with the great tugging. And the heat and the light draw ever closer. And we enter into the great blue rippling sheet of sky that seemed once only above us and now the clouds aren't above us but within us now below us now rolling ever smaller until they are as small as the breathing houses and cows once were and the only thing that we feel is each other and the great heat of the sun and there are no planes and no birds and it is just us flying right over the stump of the tree that at one point in time must have been felled. And it didn't feel like magic because it just felt obvious. The sun was speaking to us all morning and we just finally paid attention and it was able to show us its grace. It took a liking to us that day. It wanted us to notice its heat on our hands and we took notice.
    <br>
    <br>
    And slowly someone below comes huffing up the hill and we are gently released back to the stump still holding each other. And as she comes by, an old lady with proper hiking gear and sticks, she whispers out 'bonne repose' and continues on smiling gently at us as if she knows that we just noticed the light that we were just propelled deeper into the fabric of light. And then it's just us again and when I stand it's just us and the sap on my jeans and the clouds roll and the birds fly and the pines whisper and we are laughing and it's time to go back down the hill.
