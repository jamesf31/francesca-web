    Francesca is a multidisciplinary maker using found and local materials as central to form, concept and scale.
    <br>
    <br>
    <b>Artist Statement</b>
    <br>
    <br>
    I like to be efficient, above all.
<br>
My practice is always efficient. I don’t believe in wasted time. If I am working, I am really working.
<br>
However, if I am having lunch, I am quite frankly having lunch. Because I am a woman, I can also multitask (sometimes).
<br>
The efficiency I embody takes shape in a few ways.
<br>
Firstly, the material choice.
<br>
If I can find it on the floor, in a scrap bin, in a pile of rubbish on the street, then that suits me. There are more than enough materials around and so many leftovers.
<br>
Resourcefulness is testament to my multidisciplinary nature. Instead of hindering the ways I create, it inspires me. No white paper around? The project suddenly becomes cardboard. This is playful and refreshing. Sometimes even unexpected.
<br>
Secondly, the creative process.
<br>
Boiling down is important in my work. A constant spring clean is in progress, attempting to understand the relevant and irrelevant creations and streamline these iterations into considered outcomes. I create a lot and take time to reflect critically. Although it may not seem so, this is playful. I puzzle, rearrange, and enquire. Mechanical pattern making and abstracting linear data are process stages I often utilise.
<br>
Efficiency does not extend to perfect. I often fail and I often make mistakes. This makes my work better.
<br>
Above all, I love creating. I am a maker.
